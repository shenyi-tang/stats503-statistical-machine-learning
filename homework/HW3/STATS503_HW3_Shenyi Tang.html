Question 1¶

ISLP chapter 4, conceptual excercise #1

Using a little bit of algebra, prove that (4.2) $$p(X)=\cfrac{e^{\beta_0+\beta_1 X}}{1+e^{\beta_0+\beta_1 X}}$$ is equivalent to (4.3) $$\cfrac{p(X)}{1-p(X)}=e^{\beta_0+\beta_1 X}$$ In other words, the logistic function representation and logit representation for the logistic regression model are equivalent.

Let $Z = e^{\beta_0+\beta_1X}, p(X) = \frac{Z}{1+Z}$

So we can get, $$ \begin{align*} \frac{1}{p(X)} &= \frac{1+Z}{Z} = \frac{1}{Z} + 1 \\ \frac{1}{p(X)} - 1 &= \frac{1}{Z} \\ Z &= \frac{1}{\frac{1}{p(X)} - 1} = \frac{1}{\frac{1-p(X)}{p(X)}} = \frac{p(X)}{1-p(X)} = e^{\beta_0+\beta_1X} \end{align*} $$

(a) Suppose that we have a set of observations, each with measurements on $p=1$ feature, $X.$ We assume that $X$ is uniformly (evenly) distributed on $[0,1]$. This problem has to do with odds

(a) On average, what fraction of people with an odds of $0.37$ of defaulting on their credit card payment will in fact default?

$\cfrac{p(x)}{1-p(x)}=0.37 \Rightarrow p(x)\approx 0.27$

(b) Suppose that an individual has a 16 % chance of defaulting on her credit card payment. What are the odds that she will default?

$p(x)=0.16\Rightarrow \cfrac{p(x)}{1-p(x)}\approx 0.19$

Question 4¶

ISLP chapter 4, conceptual excercise #12, parts a, b, and c

Suppose that you wish to classify an observation $X \in \mathbb{R}$ into apples and oranges. You fit a logistic regression model and find that $$\widehat{Pr}(Y=\text{orange}|X=x)=\cfrac{\text{exp}(\hat{\beta_0}+\hat{\beta_1}x)}{1+\text{exp}(\hat{\beta_0}+\hat{\beta_1}x)}$$ Your friend fits a logistic regression model to the same data using $softmax$ formulation in $(4.13)$, and finds that $$ \widehat{\text{Pr}}(Y = \text{orange}|X = x) = \frac{\exp(\hat{\alpha}_{\text{orange}0} + \hat{\alpha}_{\text{orange}1}x)}{\exp(\hat{\alpha}_{\text{orange}0} + \hat{\alpha}_{\text{orange}1}x) + \exp(\hat{\alpha}_{\text{apple}0} + \hat{\alpha}_{\text{apple}1}x)}. $$

(a) What is the log odds of $\text{orange}$ versus $\text{apple}$ in your model

$$ \begin{gathered} \because log\ odds=\text{ln}(\cfrac{p(x)}{1-p(x)}) \\ \therefore log\ odds_{\text{orange vs. apple}} = \hat{\beta}_0+\hat{\beta}_1x \end{gathered} $$

(b) What is the log odds of $\text{orange}$ versus $\text{apple}$ in your friend's model

$$ \begin{gathered} \because log\ odds=\text{ln}(\cfrac{p(x)}{1-p(x)}) \\ \therefore log\ odds_{\text{orange vs. apple}} = (\hat{\alpha}_{\text{orange}0} + \hat{\alpha}_{\text{orange}1}x)-(\hat{\alpha}_{\text{apple}0} + \hat{\alpha}_{\text{apple}1}x) \end{gathered} $$

(c) Suppose that in your model, $\hat{\beta}_0=2$ and $\hat{\beta}_1=-1$. What are coefficients estimated in your friend's model? Be as specific as possible.

In my model, $log\ odds=2-x$.

Therefore in my friend's model, $\hat{\alpha}_{\text{orange}0}-\hat{\alpha}_{\text{apple}0}=2,\ \hat{\alpha}_{\text{orange}1}-\hat{\alpha}_{\text{apple}1}=-1$

Therefore, $\hat{\alpha}_{\text{orange}0}=2,\ \hat{\alpha}_{\text{orange}1}=-1,\ \hat{\alpha}_{\text{apple}0}=0, \hat{\alpha}_{\text{apple}1}=0$

Question 5¶

Suppose we fit logistic regression to predict the probability that a STATS 503 student gets an A in the class, from two variables. The variables are average hours of study per week $(X_1)$ adn GPA in other statistics courses taken $(X_2)$. The model estimates $\beta_0=-4, \beta_1=0.05, \beta_2=1$.

(a) Predict the probability of getting an A for a student who studies 5 hours a week and has a GPA of 3.5 in other statistics courses.

$$ \begin{gathered} \widehat{p(x)}=\cfrac{exp(\hat{\beta}_0+\hat{\beta}_1X_1+\hat{\beta}_2X_2)}{1+exp(\hat{\beta}_0+\hat{\beta}_1X_1+\hat{\beta}_2X_2)} \\[1em] \because X_1=5,\ X_2=3.5 \\[1em] \therefore \widehat{p(x)}=\cfrac{e^{-0.25}}{1+e^{-0.25}}\approx 0.4378 \end{gathered} $$

(b) What are the odds that this student will get an A?

$$ log\ odds=\cfrac{\widehat{p(x)}}{1-\widehat{p(x)}}=0.7788 $$

(c) How many hours does this student need to study for the model to predict a $50\%$ change of getting an A.

$$ \begin{gathered} 0.5 = \cfrac{exp(-4+\hat{\beta}_1X_1+3.5)}{1+exp(-4+\hat{\beta}_1X_1+3.5)} \\[1em] X_1=10 \end{gathered} $$

import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
thresholds = np.linspace(0, 1, 101)

fprs = []
tprs = []

for t in thresholds:
    y_pred = np.where(y_prob[:, 1] > t, 1, 0)
    
    tn = sum((y_test == 0) & (y_pred == 0))
    fp = sum((y_test == 0) & (y_pred == 1))
    fn = sum((y_test == 1) & (y_pred == 0))
    tp = sum((y_test == 1) & (y_pred == 1))
    
    fpr = fp / (fp + tn) if (fp + tn) > 0 else 0
    tpr = tp / (tp + fn) if (tp + fn) > 0 else 0
    
    fprs.append(fpr)
    tprs.append(tpr)

plt.figure(figsize=(8, 6))
plt.plot(fprs, tprs, 'b-', label='ROC curve')
plt.plot([0, 1], [0, 1], 'k--', label='Random classifier')
plt.xlabel('False Positive Rate (FPR)')
plt.ylabel('True Positive Rate (TPR)')
plt.title('ROC Curve')
plt.legend()
plt.grid(True)
plt.show()
